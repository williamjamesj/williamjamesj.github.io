Types of Encryption

Caesar Cipher

The first method (and most basic) method of encryption is the Caesar Cipher, a method that simply shifts all of the characters through the alphabet by a certain number (the key). For example, the plaintext "hello" shifted by a key of 1 would become "ifmmp", which becomes unreadable unless decrypted. These forms below ignores any non-alphanumeric characters.

Vigenère Cipher & Gronsfeld Cipher

The Vigenère Cipher is a more complicated method of encryption that typically uses a multiple character key, that can be used to encrypt data to an extent that is harder to brute force than a simple Caesar Cipher.
An example of such would be encrypting the message "hello" with the key of "dog".

Step 1: Repeat the key, so that it is long enough for the entire plaintext. For example "dog" becomes "dogdo".
Step 2: For each of the characters, use a chart like the one to the right to change each of the characters, according to its companion in the repeated key. For example: The H becomes K, the E becomes S, the L becomes R, the second L becomes O and the final O becomes C.
Step 3: Put all of the characters together to make the encrypted text: In this example "hello" becomes "ksroc" after being encrypted with the key "dog".

This makes the Vigenère cipher much more difficult to crack, as there are significantly more potential combinations than a basic Caesar Cipher.
